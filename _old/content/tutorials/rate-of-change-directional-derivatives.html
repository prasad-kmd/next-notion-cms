Question (a)

Find the maximum rate of change of $f(x,y) = xe^{-y}$ at the point $(-2,0)$.

Question (b)

Let $A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 4 \\ 2 & 3 \end{pmatrix}$.

  • Find the matrix that diagonalize matrix A.
  • Use the diagonalization of matrix A to find a formula for $A^k$ for any $k \in \mathbb{N}$.
Step-by-Step Analysis

Answer & Method for Question (a)

The maximum rate of change of a multivariable function at a point is found by calculating the magnitude of its gradient vector at that point.

Given Function & Point

$f(x,y) = xe^{-y}$
Point: $(-2,0)$
1 Partial Derivatives
$\frac{\partial f}{\partial x} = \frac{\partial}{\partial x}(xe^{-y}) = e^{-y}$
$\frac{\partial f}{\partial y} = \frac{\partial}{\partial y}(xe^{-y}) = x(-e^{-y}) = -xe^{-y}$
2 Gradient Vector
$\nabla f(x,y) = \left\langle \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}, \frac{\partial f}{\partial y} \right\rangle = \langle e^{-y}, -xe^{-y} \rangle$
3 Evaluate Gradient at Point $(-2,0)$
$\nabla f(-2,0) = \langle e^{-0}, -(-2)e^{-0} \rangle = \langle 1, 2e^0 \rangle = \langle 1, 2 \cdot 1 \rangle = \langle 1, 2 \rangle$
4 Magnitude of Gradient (Maximum Rate of Change)
$||\nabla f(-2,0)|| = \sqrt{(1)^2 + (2)^2} = \sqrt{1 + 4} = \sqrt{5}$

Result: The maximum rate of change is $\sqrt{5}$.

Fundamental Methodology

  • 1. Gradient Vector: The gradient $\nabla f$ points in the direction of the greatest rate of increase.
  • 2. Partial Derivatives: Calculate all first-order partial derivatives by treating other variables as constants.
  • 3. Evaluate: Substitute the specific point coordinates into the gradient vector.
  • 4. Magnitude: Compute the magnitude using $\sqrt{a^2 + b^2}$.

Answer & Method for Question (b)

i. Matrix Diagonalization

To diagonalize matrix A, we find its eigenvalues and corresponding eigenvectors. The matrix $P$, whose columns are the eigenvectors, is the diagonalizing matrix.

Matrix $A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 4 \\ 2 & 3 \end{pmatrix}$
1. Find Eigenvalues

Solve $\text{det}(A - \lambda I) = 0$

$\lambda^2 - 4\lambda - 5 = 0 \Rightarrow (\lambda - 5)(\lambda + 1) = 0$
$\lambda_1 = 5, \quad \lambda_2 = -1$
2. Find Eigenvectors

For $\lambda_1 = 5$

$\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}$

For $\lambda_2 = -1$

$\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{pmatrix} -2 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}$
3. Construct P
$P = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 1 \end{pmatrix}$

ii. Formula for $A^k$

If $A = PDP^{-1}$, then $A^k = PD^kP^{-1}$.

Diagonal Power

$D^k = \begin{pmatrix} 5^k & 0 \\ 0 & (-1)^k \end{pmatrix}$

Inverse Matrix

$P^{-1} = \frac{1}{3} \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ -1 & 1 \end{pmatrix}$
4. Final Computation
$A^k = \frac{1}{3} \begin{pmatrix} 5^k + 2(-1)^k & 2 \cdot 5^k - 2(-1)^k \\ 5^k - (-1)^k & 2 \cdot 5^k + (-1)^k \end{pmatrix}$

Matrix Operation Logic

Diagonalization Steps:

  • Solve Characteristic Equation
  • Compute Eigenvectors
  • Construct $P$ (vectors) and $D$ (values)

Computing Powers:

  • Apply $A^k = PD^kP^{-1}$
  • Raise $D$ entries to $k$
  • Matrix multiplication order is critical
