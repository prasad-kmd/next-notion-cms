$$\frac{\partial^{2} u}{\partial x^{2}}+\frac{\partial^{2} u}{\partial y^{2}}=0 \quad \text{on} \quad 0 \le x \le 1, 0 \le y \le 1$$

Answer & Method Analysis

Detailed Answer for Question 1

The problem involves setting up a system of equations for the approximate temperature distribution $u(x, y)$ using the finite difference method on a $1 \times 1$ plate with a square mesh ($h=k$).

Derivation Foundations:

Using central differences for second derivatives:

$$\frac{\partial^{2} u}{\partial x^{2}} \approx \frac{u_{i+1,j} - 2u_{i,j} + u_{i-1,j}}{h^2}, \quad \frac{\partial^{2} u}{\partial y^{2}} \approx \frac{u_{i,j+1} - 2u_{i,j} + u_{i,j-1}}{k^2}$$

For a square mesh ($h=k$), Laplace's equation simplifies to the 5-point stencil:

$$4u_{i,j} = u_{i+1,j} + u_{i-1,j} + u_{i,j+1} + u_{i,j-1}$$

Based on the provided figure, we apply the formula to the internal points a, b, c, and d:

Point 'a' (u₁,₁)

Neighbors: b, 100 (left), c, 50 (bottom)

$$4u_a - u_b - u_c = 150$$
Point 'b' (u₂,₁)

Neighbors: 100 (right), a, d, 100 (bottom)

$$4u_b - u_a - u_d = 200$$
Point 'c' (u₁,₂)

Neighbors: d, 200 (left), 100 (top), a

$$4u_c - u_a - u_d = 300$$
Point 'd' (u₂,₂)

Neighbors: 200 (right), c, 50 (top), b

$$4u_d - u_b - u_c = 250$$

Fundamental Methodology & Approach

  • 1 PDE Identification: Start by identifying the PDE type. Laplace's equation is elliptic, requiring a focus on steady-state boundary values.
  • 2 Grid Discretization: Map the physical space to a discrete coordinate system $(i, j)$ and determine step sizes $h$ and $k$.
  • 3 Stencil Application: Utilize the 5-point stencil (average of neighbors) to transform the differential equation into an algebraic one for every internal node.
  • 4 Boundary Integration: Carefully incorporate known values from the perimeter to define the right-hand side of your linear equations.
Technical Note: For Laplace's equation, the "explicit scheme" often refers to iterative methods like the Jacobi Iteration: $u_{i,j}^{(k+1)} = \frac{1}{4}(u_{i+1,j}^{(k)} + u_{i-1,j}^{(k)} + u_{i,j+1}^{(k)} + u_{i,j-1}^{(k)})$
