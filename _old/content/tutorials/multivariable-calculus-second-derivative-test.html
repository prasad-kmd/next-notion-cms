The Challenge

Determine the nature of all the critical points of function \(f(x,y)=e^{(-\frac{x^{3}}{3}+x-y^{2})}\).

"A classic optimization problem requiring meticulous differentiation and the construction of a Hessian matrix."

Detailed Answer & Step-by-Step Solution

To determine the nature of the critical points, we utilize the Second Derivative Test. Let \(g(x,y) = -\frac{x^{3}}{3}+x-y^{2}\), then our function is \(f(x,y) = e^{g(x,y)}\).

1 First Partial Derivatives

\[ \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} = e^{(-\frac{x^{3}}{3}+x-y^{2})} (-x^2+1) \] \[ \frac{\partial f}{\partial y} = e^{(-\frac{x^{3}}{3}+x-y^{2})} (-2y) \]

2 Finding Critical Points

Setting both partial derivatives to zero:

X-axis solve:

\( -x^2+1 = 0 \implies x = \pm 1 \)

Y-axis solve:

\( -2y = 0 \implies y = 0 \)

Resulting critical points: (1, 0) and (-1, 0).

3 Second Partial Derivatives

\[ f_{xx} = e^{g(x,y)} [(-x^2+1)^2 - 2x] \] \[ f_{yy} = e^{g(x,y)} [4y^2 - 2] \] \[ f_{xy} = e^{g(x,y)} (-2y)(-x^2+1) \]

4 Hessian Determinant Evaluation

At Critical Point (1, 0):

Let \(C = e^{2/3}\).

\(f_{xx} = -2C\)
\(f_{yy} = -2C\)
\(f_{xy} = 0\)

\(D = 4C^2 > 0\) and \(f_{xx} < 0\) → Local Maximum

At Critical Point (-1, 0):

Let \(C' = e^{-2/3}\).

\(f_{xx} = 2C'\)
\(f_{yy} = -2C'\)
\(f_{xy} = 0\)

\(D = -4(C')^2 < 0\) → Saddle Point

Fundamental Methodology

Core Theory Steps
  • • Identify Critical Points where \(\nabla f = 0\).
  • • Compute Second Partial Derivatives.
  • • Calculate the Hessian Determinant (D).
Decision Matrix (D)

\(D > 0, f_{xx} > 0\): Local Min

\(D > 0, f_{xx} < 0\): Local Max

\(D < 0\): Saddle Point

\(D = 0\): Inconclusive

Exam Mastery Tips

Chain Rule Mastery: When dealing with \(e^g\), remember \(e^g\) is always positive and common to all terms.

Algebraic Accuracy: Factor out common terms immediately to simplify the Hessian calculation.

Geometric Intuition: Visualize local maxima as peaks and saddle points as "passes" between hills.

Systematic Tracking: Use a table format for \(D\) and \(f_{xx}\) values to avoid sign errors.
