Welcome to the Faculty of Engineering Technology at The Open University of Sri Lanka (OUSL). This guide provides essential information for students navigating the 2025/2026 academic year. OUSL operates on an Open and Distance Learning (ODL) philosophy, offering unparalleled flexibility compared to traditional university systems.

"The ODL system allows you to manage your own learning pace. Understanding the credit system and graduation requirements is key to completing your degree successfully."

1. Graduation Requirements

To qualify for the award of the Bachelor of Science Honours in Engineering degree, a student must fulfil several requirements within a maximum period of 12 academic years.

Total Credit Requirement

Students must earn a minimum of 140 credits distributed across various levels and categories:

  • Minimum Credits at Level 5 & 6: 70 Credits.
  • Minimum Credits at Level 6: 30 Credits.

3. GPA Calculation Rules

The Grade Point Average (GPA) is computed by considering 80 course credits from levels 4, 5, and 6.

Priority Order for GPA Calculation:

  1. Group (i): Compulsory courses at levels 5 and 6.
  2. Group (ii): Elective courses at levels 5 and 6.
  3. Group (iii): Compulsory courses at Level 4.

Within each group, courses are arranged in descending order of their Grade Point Values (GPV). If exactly 80 credits cannot be obtained, the last course is taken as a partial credit.

4. Dean's List and Academic Honors

The Faculty of Engineering Technology recognizes high achievers through the Dean's List Award.

Dean's List Eligibility Criteria:

  • Minimum of 30 credits completed in a single academic year.
  • GPA of 3.70 or better.
  • Minimum of a C grade for all courses registered in that year.
  • No repeats (F grades) or Re-sits permitted.

Placement on the Dean's List is noted on the student's official transcript and may lead to a 24-credit tuition fee scholarship for top performers.

5. IESL Recognition & Membership

The BSc. Hons (Eng) programme is designed to meet the academic standards required by the Institution of Engineers, Sri Lanka (IESL).

Important for IESL Membership: To qualify for IESL membership after graduation, students MUST have obtained at least two credit passes (C) and one simple pass (S) in a single sitting of the GCE (A/L) examination in the Physical Science stream.
